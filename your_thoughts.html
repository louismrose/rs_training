RS Training Forum (12/03/12)

Thoughts and issues raised at the RS training forum are below.

Training courses

  • RDT courses are sometimes not tailored towards our discipline, and hence are not as useful as they could be.
  • Lack of useful training for experimental method, statistics and referencing; and for specific tools (e.g. MS Word, LaTeX, Eclipse, EndNote/CiteULike/Mendely, SPSS/R).
  • Induction activities do not necessarily suit all RSes, possibly because RSes come from different backgrounds (both academic and cultural).

Reliance on tacit information

  • Key information for RSes is scattered across the departmental and university websites and other resources (e.g. handbook).
  • It's not always clear who (in the department or university) to ask in order to solve a problem.
  • Lack of structure in introductory processes, and particularly for non-October starters.

Community

  • Lack of peer-to-peer sharing of knowledge between RSes.
  • Lack of "group unity" (community feeling?).
  • Not all RSes have opportunities to give many presentations (other than literature review seminar and thesis seminar).
