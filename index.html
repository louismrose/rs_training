Training for Research Students

Increasingly, a doctoral degree involves developing the skills necessary to become an independent researcher, and not "just" making an outstanding contribution to your scientific community.

This page contains information about the training opportunities available to Research Students in the department, is maintained by the Research Student Training Officer (currently Louis Rose), and discusses the following:

  • Training Centres and PFA (where can I receive training?)
  • Upcoming Skillsforge Events
  • Staff involved with Research Student Training (who do I speak with about training?)

Two key training activities deserve special mention:

  • the York Doctoral Symposium (a research event run by research students for research students) provides fantastic hands-on training in running and participating in research events.
  • becoming a Postgraduate Teaching Assistant (PTA) is an excellent opportunity to further develop teaching skills, and to earn a little extra money.

Training Centres

Once you have identified skills in which you wish to receive training, the following resources can help you to choose an appropriate course. If you find that there is not an appropriate course for a skill that you wish to develop, please tell us.

  • York Skillsforge (for finding and booking training courses)
  • Research Development Team (RDT) (for more information on training courses)
  • York Doctoral Symposium (YDS) (run by students for students; a fantastic opportunity for hands-on training)
  • Centre for English Language Teaching (for English language tuition)
  • Student Skills Hub (available to undergraduates and postgraduates)

Preparing Future Academics

Preparing Future Academics (PFA) is a training programme, which helps research students to further develop learning and teaching techniques. Participants develop a portfolio throughout the programme, and prizes are typically awarded for the strongest portfolios.

This is an excellent programme, and is largely unrivalled -- there is very little like this at other UK universities. Applications for the 2012/13 programme open in Spring 2012, and there has been fairly strong competition for places in previous years.

Peer-Supported Teaching Groups

Research students are invited to join the department's Peer-Supported Teaching groups, which involve discussion of teaching principles and practice. This year's groups are focusing on topics such as teaching AI to undergraduates, putting a module online and innovative methods of assessment.

Meetings of the PST groups will be fairly infrequent, and hence this is a simple way to develop and reflect on any of the teaching you may do in the department. Sign up for one or more PSTs is via this Google doc.

Upcoming Skillsforge Events

Skillsforge is a suite of online tools that assists research students in their training and evaluation of their practice. For example, you can use Skillsforge to record a research journal and to locate and register for training courses run by the university.
