Training for Research Students

Increasingly, a doctoral degree involves developing the skills necessary to become an independent researcher, and not just making an outstanding contribution to your scientific community.

This page contains information about the training opportunities available to Research Students in the department, is maintained by the Research Student Training Officer (currently Louis Rose), and discusses the following:

  • Training Centres (where can I receive training?)
  • Upcoming Training Events in the Department
  • Upcoming Skillsforge Events
  • Staff involved with Research Student Training (who do I speak with about training?)

Training Centres

Once you have identified skills in which you wish to receive training, the following resources can help you to choose an appropriate course. If you find that there is not an appropriate course for a skill that you wish to develop, please tell us.

  • York Skillsforge
  • The University's Research Development team
  • Postgraduate Certificate in Academic Practice (PGCAP) (some modules may be available to Research Students)
  • The University's Student Skills Hub (available to undergraduates and postgraduates)
  • York Doctoral Symposium (YDS) (run by students for students; a fantastic opportunity for hands-on training)

Skillsforge is a suite of online tools that assists research students in their training and evaluation of their practice.
