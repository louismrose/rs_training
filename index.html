Key training activities

The York Doctoral Symposium is a research event run by research students for research students. YDS provides fantastic hands-on training in running and participating in research events.

Taking a Postgraduate Teaching Assistant (PTA) post is an excellent opportunity to further develop teaching skills, and to earn a little extra money.

What kinds of training are available?

As you identify skills in which you wish to receive training, you should investigate the following training activities and options. Please tell us if there is no support for a particular skill that you wish to develop.

  • York Skillsforge (for finding and booking training courses)
  • Research Development Team (RDT) (for more information on training courses)
  • York Doctoral Symposium (YDS) (run by students for students; a fantastic opportunity for hands-on training)
  • Centre for English Language Teaching (for English language tuition)
  • Student Skills Hub (available to undergraduates and postgraduates)

Preparing Future Academics

Preparing Future Academics (PFA) is a training programme that helps research students to further develop learning and teaching techniques. Participants develop a portfolio throughout the programme, and prizes are typically awarded for the strongest portfolios.

This is an excellent programme, and is largely unrivalled - there is very little like this at other UK universities. Applications for the 2012/13 programme open in Spring 2012, and there has been fairly strong competition for places in previous years.

Peer-Supported Teaching Groups

Research students are invited to join the department's Peer-Supported Teaching groups, which involve discussion of teaching principles and practice. This year's groups are focusing on topics such as teaching AI to undergraduates, putting a module online and innovative methods of assessment.

Meetings of the PST groups will be fairly infrequent, and hence this is a simple way to develop and reflect on any of the teaching you may do in the department. Sign up for one or more PSTs is via this Google doc.
